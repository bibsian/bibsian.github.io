Metrics - Short Short Course

These questions should be applied to all metrics in my opinion; metrics for various subsystem’s performance, business metrics, product metrics, etc.

Always ask yourself the following:

  1. What am I trying to measure and how can I describe & communicate it?
    • What does it mean from the perspectives of others e.g. the user’s view, your data, web, and business team’s view, etc.?
    • Can I measure it accurately?
    • Is it worth spending time to measure it more accurately?
    • Can I drive this metric or control it with interventions?
    • How is it going to be used by the business?
      • This one is suuuper important to understand because it’ll inform whether you should even spend time on it at all.

In Conclusion

The more important the measurement is to the business the more tech effort you should put into aligning all your teams on the data expectations. This includes coming to agreements on how the data and metrics should be accessed, what’s the expected quality, how far back, what are the assumptions, and maybe a few other things.
